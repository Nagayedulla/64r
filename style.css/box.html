HANUMAN

Hanuman is a Hindu deity, the devoted vanara (monkey-like being) central to the epic Ramayana, known for his immense strength, wisdom, and unwavering devotion to Lord Rama. Also called Anjaneya (son of the wind god Vayu), he possesses divine powers like shapeshifting and flying, which he uses to aid Rama in rescuing Sita from Ravana. Hanuman symbolizes courage, self-discipline, and selfless service, inspiring devotees worldwide through his heroic feats and virtuous

Lord venkateshwara

Lord Venkateswara, also known as Balaji, Srinivasa, and Venkatachalapati, is a popular Hindu deity and a form of Lord Vishnu. He resides in the famous temple on the Tirumala Hills in Andhra Pradesh and is revered as the "Lord who destroys sins" and a provider of prosperity and divine mercy. His temple is a major pilgrimage site, and devotees offer wealth to help him repay a loan taken for his wedding to Goddess Lakshmi, who took birth as Padmavathi

shiva

Lord Shiva is a principal deity in Hinduism, revered as the destroyer within the Trimurti (Trinity) and the "God of Gods" (Mahadev). He embodies paradox, serving as the transformer who destroys evil and chaos to facilitate liberation and renewal, rather than pure malevolence. Worshipped by Shaivas, Shiva is depicted in various forms like a meditating yogi, the cosmic dancer Nataraja, and Ardhanarishvara (half-man, half-woman) to represent his multifaceted nature as the supreme power, the source of knowledge, and the protector of his devotees
